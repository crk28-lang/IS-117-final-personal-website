# Swedish Cottage Style: A Design History

A comprehensive digital study of the history, principles, and contemporary applications of Swedish cottage design, presented in a professional, RISD-inspired aesthetic.

## Project Overview

This website explores Swedish cottage style (stuga) from its origins in 17th-century rural Sweden through its influence on contemporary global design. The project covers the history, design principles, and practical applications of one of Scandinavia's most significant design movements.

## Website Structure

### Core Pages

- **index.html** - Landing page with overview and navigation to all sections
- **history.html** - Comprehensive history from 17th century to present
- **examples-furniture.html** - Analysis of iconic Swedish furniture forms and construction techniques
- **examples-color.html** - The distinctive Swedish cottage color palette and natural pigment sources
- **examples-architecture.html** - Building design, structural principles, and regional variations
- **examples-textiles.html** - Traditional weaving, dyeing, patterns, and Kurbits decoration
- **modern-uses.html** - Contemporary applications in design, architecture, and culture
- **styles.css** - Professional, minimalist styling inspired by RISD design principles

## Design Philosophy

The website's design reflects the very principles it documents:

- **Minimalism**: Clean typography and layout without unnecessary ornamentation
- **Functionality**: Intuitive navigation and clear information hierarchy
- **Natural Materials Aesthetic**: Warm color palette derived from historical Swedish cottage colors
- **Accessibility**: Responsive design, readable typography, and organized content
- **Craft Values**: Hand-coded HTML and CSS emphasizing semantic structure

## Color Palette

The website uses traditional Swedish cottage colors:

- **Falun Red** (#c9806e) - Accent color symbolizing Swedish design
- **Forest Green** (#7a9b8e) - Cool, muted tone connecting to natural landscape
- **Primary Dark** (#2c3e3f) - Deep blue-black for headers and structure
- **Cream/Off-white** (#f5f3f0) - Primary background reflecting historical interiors

## Key Content Areas

### Historical Foundation
Traces the evolution of Swedish cottage style from practical rural design to influential global aesthetic movement, documenting how necessity spawned enduring beauty.

### Design Elements
- **Furniture**: Iconic forms like Mora clocks, stools, and storage pieces
- **Colors**: Natural pigment sources and regional color traditions
- **Architecture**: Building structures, interior organization, and regional variations
- **Textiles**: Weaving techniques, natural dyes, and Kurbits patterns

### Contemporary Relevance
Documents how historical principles inform:
- Modern Scandinavian interior design
- Sustainable design practices
- Craft and artisanal movements
- Architecture and heritage preservation
- Graphic and product design
